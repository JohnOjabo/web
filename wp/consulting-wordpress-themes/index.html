What To Look For In A Consulting WordPress Theme?

1. Place your services front and center

You must place your amazing services in the spotlight, so everyone can quickly access them.

Remember, your visitors need quick solutions. Otherwise, they’ll leave the website early.

Start with sharing the necessary details on the home page but you can then go in-depth with your dedicated service pages.

Call-to-actions are also valuable because visitors can immediately act after finding their needs.

2. Contacts, pricing & other business information

Regarding a consulting business website, you must ensure contact, details, and information are visible. (Google Maps to showcase your location is also of great value.)

That’s not something to consider with a dedicated consulting WordPress theme.

The tool has special sections covering all the must-haves (with many extras). For the most part, you only need to add your info and activate the elements you need.

Of course, you can also tweak things to brand them (but that’ll take only a few clicks).

3. Online bookings and appointments

Less and less folks rely on email and phone to make an appointment or book your consulting services.

Instead, they prefer being in control from the comfort of their desktop or mobile device by accessing an online calendar (to pick their ideal date and time).

Here’s also where WooCommerce comes into play, so those interested can pay upfront.

All this helps the owner stay more organized, saving time to answer calls and emails.

4. Testimonials and case studies

Including client testimonials and case studies is an excellent way to build trust and credibility.

Displaying positive feedback on the home page is the perfect location, so everyone can see what others say about you without jumping to other pages.

But you can devote an exclusive website section for your successful case studies. Present each on an individual page, and feel free to share all the details in-depth.

5. High-quality design

To your luck, any consulting WordPress themes we have here promise you a professional outcome.

There is no need to build anything from scratch, and you also do not need to have experience with coding and design.

With that in mind, you need to invest just a few minutes to configure the default theme and set it according to your needs.

6. Page builder compatibility

One of the fastest ways to modify and enhance the default web design is the convenient drag and drop page builder.

This instantly unlocks the complete creative freedom you deserve.

Two of the most popular WP page builders are Elementor and WPBakery. You can introduce your creative touch and spice things up with your original branding FAST.

7. Mobile-ready, fast loading and SEO-optimized layout

Any consulting website needs to be responsive. This is an absolute must for the success you would like to achieve.

Luckily, we gathered only mobile-friendly WP themes, so you don’t need to worry about the technicalities.

Not just that, but the overall performance of your consulting website must also be on point. We are speaking about fast loading speed, heck, even SEO.

8. Consulting blog

Rarely does anyone talk about adding an insightful blog to a consulting website, but we think it’s SO necessary.

You can use it as a hub to share tips and tricks, get on a more personal level with your audience, showcase your work process, and more.

Of course, an optimized blog can bring you many new clients that would otherwise never reach you.

Don’t be afraid to share your knowledge (but don’t share too much).

      Other things such as FAQ, Profile, Specials Deals, Contact Page, Quotes are up to you to create. By that I don’t mean that you have to code something but you have to use WordPress code functionality such as posts, pages categories, tags to create these things. That’t the basic things for creating website based on WordPress. My personal recommendation is Consulting theme but other themes will work just as well. The main thing is to concentrate on theme design and not functionality as that’s what WordPress plugins and WordPress core itself is for.
